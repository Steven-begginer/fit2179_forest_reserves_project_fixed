The Changing Map of Malaysia’s Forest Reserves

A story-driven visualisation about how Malaysia’s forest reserve areas changed from 2003 to 2021, why state-level differences matter, and how these patterns connect with population and rainfall/flood context.

DomainEnvironmental protection
AudienceGeneral Malaysian public
Main needMake state-level forest change visible

Why this visualisation?

National totals can hide regional differences. This webpage uses maps and diagrams to show which states hold the largest forest reserves, which states gained or lost protected forest area, and why these changes deserve public attention.

07

Population context

Forest reserve change is easier to understand when we also see where people are concentrated.

08

Forest area and population

This comparison helps reveal whether states with larger populations also hold larger forest reserve areas.

09

Forest reserve per person

Normalising by population makes the story more relatable to a general audience.

10

Rainfall and flood context

This chart adds environmental context. It does not prove that forest reserve change causes floods.

Key takeaway

Malaysia’s forest reserve story is uneven. A national trend is useful, but the state-level view reveals the real geography of forest protection: where reserve areas are concentrated, where they changed most, and how these patterns relate to people and environmental pressure.
